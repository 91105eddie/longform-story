## Role Findings

### Showrunner

The story has a clear intellectual and moral foundation. The next required decision is format: pilot episode, short film, concept trailer, or proof-of-concept sequence.

The current protagonist starts in 2026 with a debt and responsibility crisis, while the world story formally starts in 2030. This can work if the project uses him as a personal bridge into the AI productivity explosion. It needs an explicit decision:

- Is he the long-term protagonist?
- Is he a prologue protagonist?
- Does his personal failure become a mirror of civilization's later failure?

### Worldbuilding Designer

The world has strong macro logic: AI productivity, mass unemployment, utopian governance, stability as highest morality, technical aristocracy, district rule, and entertainment control.

The missing layer is operational detail. For animation, the audience needs to see how the system appears in daily life:

- what an AI API looks like in a normal workplace,
- what people lose first,
- what the public is told,
- what data or interface makes power visible,
- how Gongheng is foreshadowed before it becomes the center of governance.

### Character Writer

The protagonist is currently the most developed part of the project. His flaw fits the theme: he uses narratives, credit, and future promises to avoid present accountability.

The main gap is cast architecture. The project needs at least three supporting roles for the first unit:

- someone harmed by the protagonist's past promises,
- someone who sees AI as salvation,
- someone who profits from or administers the new AI workflow.

These roles should not only explain the world. They should force the protagonist to choose.

### Plot Architect

The macro outline is coherent, but the first unit needs a sharper dramatic container.

Recommended first-unit structure:

1. Opening: protagonist faces a concrete debt or credibility crisis.
2. Inciting incident: an AI system removes or transforms a job, income source, or human role he depends on.
3. Escalation: he tries to use AI as a way out, but the tool also makes avoidance easier.
4. Turn: he realizes the same logic that can save him personally may make people politically unnecessary.
5. Ending: he chooses either to face one concrete responsibility or to use the new system to escape, setting his arc direction.

### Production Manager

The project is ready for structured development work, not production. The correct next milestone is:

> First animated unit package v0.1

Required contents:

- format decision,
- first-unit premise,
- protagonist decision,
- scene list,
- animation script draft,
- initial visual style guide,
- first shot list.

4. Supporting characters are underdeveloped.
5. Gongheng's early visible form is not defined.
6. The project has no animation script format yet.
7. The visual direction is not yet specific enough for concept art or storyboard.

## Suggested First Decision

Recommended format for the first milestone:

> 8-12 minute animated proof-of-concept episode.

Reason:

- A full series pilot requires too many unresolved commitments.
- A concept trailer may look attractive but can avoid hard story problems.
- A short proof-of-concept forces the project to solve character, world, scenes, visuals, and production needs at manageable scale.
